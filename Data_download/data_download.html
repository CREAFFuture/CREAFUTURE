Resources to get data sets

Generalist datasets

  • R comes with some standard data sets, check “data()” or the datasets package. It is common for R packages to contain data sets that demonstrate functions.

Repositories of ecological datasets

  • Knowledge Network for Biodiversity (KNB) is an international repository of ecological data sets. At the time of writing (24th March 2023) it stores over 28,000 datasets., with uneven geographical coverage. Find a light introduction to its use here.
  • Environmental Data Initiative (EDI) archives a lot of environmental data that comes from publicly-funded research, in particular, Long-Term Ecological research (LTER) in the USA. Find a light introduction to its use here.
  • National Ecological Observatory Network (NEON) is a network of field sites across the USA that regularly collect ecological data. Find a light introduction to its use here.

Repositories for species occurrence

  • Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF)
  • Ocean Biodiversity Information System (OBIS): like GBIF for marine species. Find a light introduction to its use here.

Repositories of repositories

Indeed, some webpages aggregate or provide convenient lists of pre-existing repositories,

  • DataONE aggregates data sets from several repositories and organizations (including the already mentioned KNB, EDI and NEON). At the time of writing (24th March 2023) it stores over 840,000 datasets, with uneven geographical coverage.
  • Central Michigan University Library hosts a great list of resources, including ecological, organismal and environmental data.
